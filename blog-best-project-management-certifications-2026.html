Best Project Management Certifications 2026: PMP, Scrum & More

Project management is one of Chicago's most in-demand skill sets, with PM roles across construction, finance, healthcare, and tech paying $90,000-$160,000. The right certification can increase your salary by 20-30% and open doors at major Chicago employers like Deloitte, Accenture, McKinsey, and the city's Fortune 500 companies.

PMP — Project Management Professional ($555 exam)

The gold standard PM certification from PMI (Project Management Institute). Required by most enterprise employers in Chicago's Loop. Requires 36 months of project management experience (with a degree) or 60 months (without). The exam covers predictive (waterfall), agile, and hybrid methodologies. Median salary boost: +$25,000. PMI's Chicago chapter (PMI-Chicagoland) offers study groups and networking events.

CAPM — Certified Associate in PM ($300 exam)

Entry-level PMI certification ideal for career switchers. Requires 23 hours of PM education (available online for $200-$500) but no professional experience. Covers the same PMBOK framework as PMP at a foundational level. Perfect stepping stone if you're transitioning into PM from another Chicago career.

Scrum Master Certifications

CSM — Certified ScrumMaster ($995-$1,495 including course): From Scrum Alliance. Requires a 2-day course (available in Chicago from multiple providers) plus a simple exam. Most popular agile certification. Chicago's tech companies (Grubhub, Tempus, Avant, Braintree) strongly prefer CSM-certified Scrum Masters.

PSM I — Professional Scrum Master ($150 exam): From Scrum.org. No course required — just pass the exam. More rigorous exam than CSM with an 85% passing threshold. Respected equally by employers. Much cheaper path if you can self-study.
